To the best mom in the world

Brooklyn's Reflections on Mom

By Brooklyn, age 20  ·  Mother's Day 2026

The first thing that pops into my head when I think of Mom…

"When I went to college, we grew a lot closer!"

🌸

The Woman You Are

Something I've watched grow in you that takes my breath away…

She started to love herself again!! It's been so beautiful to witness.

Something I admire and hope to have myself one day…

She doesn't take shit from anyone. I hope to be more confident with that one day.

Something I notice changing in you…

She has become more sure of herself. That's cool to see.

Something I'm proud of you for getting through…

A lot of trauma and stress, but she handles herself very well.

✨

What You've Taught Me

The best advice you've ever given me…

There's three sides to every story. It saved me from a lot of heartbreak.

Another piece of advice I carry with me…

It's ok to leave someone that doesn't benefit you. The only person you can always rely on is yourself.

Something I get now that I didn't as a kid…

Change. It was my biggest fear as a kid, but she always helped me through it.

When I have kids someday, I want to raise them the way you raised me…

I hope I can raise my kids to be as headstrong and hardworking as you made me. A little kinder than I was.

💝

Sacrifices I See

A sacrifice you made that I'll never forget…

She took me to Chuck E. Cheese even when we were broke, because she knew how much I loved it.

Something that showed me how much you cared…

She supported me at every cheer competition and was the loudest parent there.

A moment I'm proud of you for…

Leaving a very bad situation and going to the military for me!

🌿

Memories I'll Keep Forever

A childhood memory I always come back to…

Our mommy and me dates when I was little!

Something I wish you gave yourself more of…

I think she would give herself a little more grace.

Something we're both still figuring out together…

We had to learn how to deal with emotions in our own ways. We're both learning how we can handle different emotions in a healthier way.

⭐

Only You

A phrase I'll definitely catch myself saying someday…

"One day you're going to want pots and pans for Christmas."

What I want the world to know about you…

That she is beautiful and is so loved!

😂 The funniest thing you've ever done…

Her yelling at the Alexa like a maniac.

What I want to thank you for…

For growing up with me. Even though we fought a lot, you gave me a very good childhood.

Mom, the thing I want you to know is…

"…Oma and Brunie would be so proud of the mom and woman you are today."

With all my love, Brooklyn 🌸
